Call of the Netherdeep

An Orientation Guide for Adventurers

The Kryn Dynasty

Dominating the wastes of Xhorhas, the Kryn Dynasty was founded by the drow queen Leylas Kryn—the Bright Queen—who led her people away from the Underdark and the rule of Lolth the Spider Queen. Today, it is a diverse nation where drow, orcs, goblinoids, tieflings, and humans live as equals. Many roam the wastes as nomads in clans, hunting Xhorhasian megafauna.

The Settlement of Jigow

Your journey begins in Jigow, a coastal settlement formed by a string of villages. Governed by two elders—a goblin and an orc—it is a place of friendly rivalry. The Dynasty's military, the Aurora Watch, maintains a presence here under the command of Taskhand Durth Mirimm.

"The Dynasty stands united under the light of the Luxon."

The Festival of Merit

Townsfolk in Jigow are competitive by nature. You have arrived for the Festival of Merit, where you will share the grounds with another group of local aspiring heroes.

Ayo Jabe (Water Genasi): A headstrong hunter and leader. She is impulsive and protective of her companions.
Dermot Wurder (Goblin): A thoughtful cleric who prioritises the safety of his friends. He is the emotional anchor.
Galsariad Ardyth (Drow): A wizard with a keen mind and an interest in history. He carries a certain dry wit.
Irvan Wastewalker (Human): A nomad who has travelled the wastes. He is laid-back and skilled with a blade.
Maggie Keeneyes (Ogre): A tactical strategist who enjoys poetry. She is the steady pillar of her team.
"In Jigow, competition is the language of friendship."

The Luxon & Consecution

The official deity of the Dynasty is the Luxon, an entity of rebirth. Through Consecution, souls are prepared for rebirth, gaining knowledge through many lifetimes.

Rebirth: If a consecuted follower dies within 100 miles of a Luxon beacon, their soul is ensnared and reincarnated within a newborn nearby.

Character Questions

  • What's one small heroic deed you've already accomplished?
  • Do you give your trust easily, or do people have to earn it?
  • What do you want more than anything else? How will adventuring help?

Setting Your Goals

Think about what drives your character forward. Consider creating goals across three timescales:

  • Short-term goals should be easily attainable within the next few sessions—things like earning enough coin to buy better armour, proving yourself in the Festival of Merit, or making a good impression on one of the rival adventurers.
  • Medium-term goals require more effort and time—perhaps establishing a reputation in Jigow, uncovering a piece of your character's mysterious past, or mastering a particular skill or spell.
  • Long-term goals can be grand and even vague—becoming a legendary hero, discovering the truth about the Luxon, defeating a great evil, or changing the fate of the Dynasty itself.
You don't need to write these down here, but keep them in mind as your character grows. Goals can change as your story unfolds!
Character Tip: The wastes are rugged. Athletics and Survival will serve you well in the days to come.
